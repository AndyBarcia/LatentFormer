# Journal — 2026-04-27

## Daily summary

Today focused on seed-selection behavior in LatentFormer, especially whether the no-seed Hungarian family can make Golden Seed Selection reliable enough to use as the main practical target before returning to clustering-based selection. The clearest outcome is that GT Oracle seed selection remains strong while Golden and especially Clustering still lag, but two directions look meaningfully better than the rest: preventing seed queries from participating in aggregation and the pattern-loss plus seed-masking variant that is still improving. An interactive visualization tool was also added to support qualitative inspection.

## Research focus

- understanding why seed selection succeeds under GT Oracle but fails under Golden and Clustering selection
- comparing no-seed Hungarian variants for Golden Seed Selection
- checking whether matching and aggregation design choices are the main bottlenecks
- checking whether supervising matched-query aggregation weights helps more than relying only on `loss_seed_sig`
- inspecting whether qualitative tools can help explain collapsed or misclassified query behavior

Here, "no-seed Hungarian" means the Hungarian assignment does not use the predicted seed value as part of the matching cost; matching is driven only by signature similarity, using cosine distance in the main variants tested today.

Here, "pattern loss" refers to no longer supervising matched queries only through `loss_seed_sig`; instead, the aggregation weights of the matched queries are also supervised.

## Key conclusions

- GT Oracle seed selection works much better than Golden or Clustering selection, so the current bottleneck is seed selection quality rather than the full downstream pipeline.
- Golden Seed Selection is the right near-term target; Clustering Seed Selection should wait until Golden works reliably.
- In the "no-seed Hungarian" family, removing predicted seed value from the Hungarian cost is better aligned with today's conclusions than trying to optimize matching with the seed prediction itself.
- Using the predicted seed value for matching appears harmful overall, even if it lowers seed loss.
- Supervising matched queries through their aggregation-weight patterns, not only through `loss_seed_sig`, looks like a promising direction.
- Preventing seed queries from participating in aggregation is currently the strongest confirmed direction for Golden PQ.
- Pattern loss with seed masking is promising and still improving, so it remains a serious contender.
- Extra seed cls/mask distillation losses did not help and likely make optimization worse.
- Weight dropout in aggregation did not show a meaningful effect.
- Length-normalizing query mask embeddings did not help Golden or Clustering selection and hurt GT Oracle selection.
- It remains an open problem how to let the model reduce `loss_seed_sig` and `loss_seed_weight` further; both are still relatively high in the promising runs, and this is likely related to the weak Golden Seed Selection performance.
- Undetaching GT signatures appears harmful; GT signatures should be supervised only through classification and mask losses.

## Decisions made

- Focus next on making Golden Seed Selection work as well as possible.
- Treat Clustering Seed Selection as a later follow-up, likely after threshold tuning once Golden is stable.
- Keep pursuing both `no_agg_for_seeds` and `pattern_loss_seed_masking`.
- Avoid using predicted seed value for matching.
- Treat aggregation weight dropout and length-normalized query mask embeddings as failed directions for now.
- Treat reduction of `loss_seed_sig` and `loss_seed_weight` as an unresolved optimization target.
- Avoid undetaching GT signatures.
- Drop the extra seed cls/mask distillation line unless later evidence suggests otherwise.

## Open questions

- Why is the classification loss so low while Golden Seed Selection still misclassifies easy examples?
- How can the model be allowed to lower `loss_seed_sig` and `loss_seed_weight` further?
- Would removing class information from the GT encoder improve Golden selection?
- Should the architecture explicitly separate aggregation-only queries from seed-only queries?
- Are some queries collapsing into clusters, and would a query-separation or orthogonality loss help?
- If `no_agg_for_seeds` is helping this much, is the core issue interference between aggregation and seed roles rather than matching alone?
